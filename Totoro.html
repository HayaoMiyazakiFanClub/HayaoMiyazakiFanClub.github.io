"My Neighbor Totoro" is a 1988 Japanese animated fantasy film directed by Hayao Miyazaki. It follows the story of two sisters, Satsuki and Mei, who move to the countryside with their father.

There, they encounter magical creatures, including the lovable forest spirit called Totoro. The film celebrates the wonder of childhood, the joy of exploring nature, and the importance of family and friendship.

It explores themes of animism, environmentalism, and the connection between humans and the natural world. "My Neighbor Totoro" is widely beloved and has become an iconic film in the world of animation.
