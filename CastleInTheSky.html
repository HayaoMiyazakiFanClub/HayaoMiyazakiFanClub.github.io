"Castle in the Sky" is a 1986 Japanese animated film directed by Hayao Miyazaki and animated by Studio Ghibli. It explores the themes of environmentalism and the relationships between humanity, nature, and technology.

The movie is known for its beautiful animation, imaginative world-building, and compelling characters. It is considered one of Miyazaki's masterpieces and a classic of Japanese animation.

The story follows orphans Sheeta and Pazu as they are pursued by various factions who seek Sheeta's crystal necklace, which holds the key to the legendary flying castle, Laputa.
