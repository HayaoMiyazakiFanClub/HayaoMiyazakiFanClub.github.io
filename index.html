Miyazaki Hayao is a Japanese animator, filmmaker, and manga artist. Also, he is co-founder of Studio Ghibli.

In 1963, after graduating from college, Miyazaki was employed at Toei Animation and started his career.
Miyazaki left Toei Animation in August 1971, and was hired at A-Pro.
In 1973, moving from A-Pro to Zuiyō Eizō, he and Takahata worked on World Masterpiece Theater. In 1978, Miyazaki directed the television series Future Boy Conan, this is his first TV series.

Film

In 1979, he direct his first feature anime film -- the Castle of Cagliostro.

Thereafter, Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind was released in 1984.
This film is adapted from his comic of the same name.

Shortly after, in 1985, he and other founded the animation production company Studio Ghibli.
And, the first film for this company is Laputa: Castle in the Sky.

Since then, he has created many excellent works, which are all familiar to everyone.
